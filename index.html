How the game-by-game scoring works

Game-by-game confidence-stake scoring. Each player ranked every fixture 1 (least confident) to 24 (most confident). Correct result (Home/Draw/Away) scores +stake; an exact correct score doubles that to +stake×2; a wrong result costs −stake. The winner of each matchweek gets a +5 bonus, awarded once every fixture in that matchweek has a result — it won't appear or flip mid-week. While a matchweek is still open, whoever's currently pace-setting is shown with a green outline (no points yet); it fills in solid green with the +5 once that matchweek is done. Ties for the win are broken by, in order: most exact/correct scores that matchweek, then highest matchweek points if exact scores weren't doubled — anyone still tied after that shares the bonus. Type the actual score into a fixture as results come in — every player's points and the leaderboard update instantly and are saved in this browser.

"Fetch live scores" pulls finished results from Wikipedia's World Cup group pages — free, no login, usually updated within minutes of full-time (not second-by-second). Wikipedia is treated as the source of truth: it fills in any blank fixture it has a confirmed score for, and corrects any entry — fetched or typed in — that doesn't match the official result. Fixtures Wikipedia hasn't confirmed yet are left untouched.

Scores you type in are saved automatically to this browser (localStorage) — they won't sync across devices. All Matchweek 1 results and Matchweek 2 results through 19 June 2026 are pre-filled; Japan v Tunisia (20 June) couldn't be confirmed from a reliable source in time so it's left blank — check and enter it yourself once you know the score.
